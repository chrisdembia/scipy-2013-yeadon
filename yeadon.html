Background

The human is most often modeled as a collection of rigid bodies.

Inertial Properties
mass, center of mass, moments/products of inertia

Use cases

  • simulation of human movement
  • walking/running studies
  • sports biomechanics
  • accident reconstruction
  • prosthesis design

Methods

  • Cadaver measurements: [Dempster1955], [Clauser1969], [Chandler1975]
  • Photogrammetry: [Jensen1978]
  • Ray scanning techniques: [Zatsiorsky1983], [Zatsiorsky1990]
  • MRI: [Pearsall1994], [Cheng2000]
  • Water displacement [Park1999]
  • Geometrical estimation [Yeadon 1990]

Yeadon's Method

  • The simulation of aerial movement--I. The determination of orientation angles from film data Journal of Biomechanics, 1990, 23, 59 - 66
  • The Simulation of Aerial Movement-II. A Mathematical Inertia Model of the Human Body Journal of Biomechanics, 1990, 23, 67-74
  • The simulation of aerial movement--III. The determination of the angular momentum of the human body Journal of Biomechanics, 1990, 23, 75 - 83
  • The simulation of aerial movement--IV. A computer simulation model Journal of Biomechanics, 1990, 23, 85 - 89
